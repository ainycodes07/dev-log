# My 190K Mistake: What a 4-Year CS Degree Didn’t Teach Me

I spent four years of my life and over **190,000 PKR** to get a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science. I did what I was told: I attended classes, passed my exams, and got the degree. I thought that once I had that paper in my hand, my career would start.

I was wrong.

The day I graduated, I realized I had no actual skills to survive in the real world. I had the theory, but I couldn't build anything that a company actually wanted to buy or use.

## > Two Years of Nothing
The most painful part came after graduation. I spent two years jobless. I applied for roles, showed my Final Year Project (FYP), and waited. But in the professional world, my university project wasn't enough. No one saw it as "real" work. It didn't get me a job, and it didn't even get me an internship. 

I was a "Computer Scientist" on paper, but in practice, I was a beginner who didn't know how the industry worked.

## > If I Could Go Back
If I could go back to the start of those four years, I would give myself a very different plan. I wouldn’t just focus on getting good grades. I would tell myself:

&nbsp; &nbsp; **🠊 &nbsp; Focus on Skills, Not Syllabus:** Spend those four years actually learning how to code, how to design, and how to build things that work.

&nbsp; &nbsp; **🠊 &nbsp; Do the Free Work:** Don't wait for a high-paying job. Work for free, help someone with their website, or contribute to open projects. That "free" work is what actually builds your resume.

&nbsp; &nbsp; **🠊 &nbsp; Build a Portfolio of Experience:** Use your time to create a collection of real projects. A live website that people can actually visit is worth more than any grade on a transcript.

## > The Truth About the Industry
What I learned the hard way is that a degree is just a piece of paper. It might get you through the door in some places, but it won’t help you keep the job. In the real world, people only care about three things: 

&nbsp; &nbsp; **🠊 &nbsp; What can you do?** 

&nbsp; &nbsp; **🠊 &nbsp; What have you built?** 

&nbsp; &nbsp; **🠊 &nbsp; Do you have the experience to solve our problems?**

I wasted a lot of time waiting for my degree to "save" me. Now, I know better. Whether you are a student or a graduate, don’t rely on your university to make you successful. 

The degree is not the goal. The skills, the portfolio, and the hands-on experience are the only things that truly count. Stop chasing the paper and start building your future with your own hands.
